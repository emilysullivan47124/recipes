Nacho Layer Dip

All of your favorite nacho toppings in a dippable form. The perfect appetizer for a pitch-in or cook-out. Vegetarian friendly with the right refried beans.

Ingredients

  • 1 (15 ounce) can spicy refried beans
  • 1 cup sour cream
  • 2/3 cup mayonnaise
  • 1 (1 ounce) package taco seasoning mix
  • 8 ounces salsa
  • 2 cups shredded lettuce
  • 1/2 cup diced red onion
  • 2 cups shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1 bunch green onions, chopped
  • 2 cups tomatoes, chopped
  • 3 tablespoons chopped fresh cilantro
  • Tortilla chips for serving

Directions

In a 13 x 9-inch glass serving dish, spread the refried beans.

Mix together the sour cream, mayonnaise and seasoning mix. Spread this on top of the beans.

Put on a layer of the salsa, then the shredded lettuce, then the red onion.

Mix together the shredded cheese, green onions, and chopped tomatoes. Sprinkle this on top.

Garnish with the chopped fresh cilantro.

Cover and chill for up to 12 hours. Serve with tortilla chips.

Baked Stuffed Shrimp

This recipe comes from my best friend's childhood home in Rhode Island.

Ingredients

  • 1 sleeve Ritz crackers
  • 1 can crab meat
  • 2 teaspoon paprika
  • 2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 2 teaspoon onion powder
  • 2 teaspoon Italian seasoning
  • Mayonnaise
  • 1 pound jumbo shrimp, tails on

Directions

Crush up crackers and pour into a medium bowl. Stir in spices, and mix thoroughly.

Open crab meat, and pour juices over cracker crumbs. Pick though meat, discarding any cartilage. Add meat to bowl.

Add mayo one spoonful at a time, until mixture just sticks together. Taste, and adjust seasoning as needed.

Line cookie sheet with aluminum foil.

Butterfly shrimp and place cut-side down on cookie sheet.

Top with ~1 tablespoon of crab mixture, and gently press tail into top of crab. Repeat with remaining shrimp.

Bake in 350 degree oven for 10 minutes, or until shrimp is cooked.

Carmelized Red Onion and Goat Cheese Crostini

Crusty bread, creamy cheese, melt-in-your-mouth caramelized onion. What's not to love?

Ingredients

  • 4 tablespoons olive oil, divided
  • 1 long loaf French bread, sliced into rounds
  • 1 medium red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 (5 ounce) log spreadable goat cheese
  • 2 tablespoons Balsamic vinegar
  • Salt and Pepper to taste
  • Fresh thyme sprigs for garnish

Directions

Heat 2 tablespoons olive oil over medium heat in a large skillet. Add red onion and sprinkle with salt and pepper. Saute, stirring frequently, until caramelized. Be patient, as this can take a half hour. Add Balsamic vinegar, and cook an additiona 5 minutes.

Preheat oven to 350 degrees. Place bread slices on a greased baking sheet, and brush both sides lightly with olive oil. Bake for 15-20 minutes, turning halfway through the cooking time.

Let the crostinis cool for a few minutes, and then spread each one with a thin layer of goat cheese. Top with a little dollop of the caramelized onion, and garnish with the fresh thyme if desired.

Pork Loin with Marsala Sauce

Tender roast pork with a Marsala cream sauce.

Ingredients

  • 4 pound pork loin
  • 5 tablespoons Dijon mustard, divided
  • 1 tsp dried rosemary
  • 1 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 2 shallots, minced
  • 1 cup Marsala wine
  • 1 cup heavy cream

Directions

Preheat oven to 450 degrees. In an oven-safe pan on the stove, heat oil over medium-high heat.

Rub pork with 4 tablespoons mustard, and sprinkle with rosemary. Brown pork in pan, 5 minutes per side. Slide pan into the oven, and roast for 10 minutes. Reduce heat to 350 degrees and continue cooking for 60 to 80 minutes, or until the internal temperature reaches 150 degrees. Let rest for 15 minutes before carving.

Meanwhile, melt butter in a saucepan over medium heat. Add shallots and cook until lightly brown, stirring often. Add remaining tablespoon mustard, Marsala wine, and heavy cream. Cook, stirring often, until volume has reduced by half.

Slice pork, and serve with sauce.

Thai Coconut Shrimp Curry

Shrimp in a peanut coconut sauce.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 4 tablespoons oil, divided
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes
  • 1 onion, peeled and sliced thinly pole to pole
  • 2 red or yellow bell peppers, thinly sliced
  • 1 (14 oz) can coconut milk
  • 4 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup peanut butter
  • 2 tablespoons lime juice
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 2 teaspoons ground ginger
  • 2 teaspoons dried basil
  • Cooked rice to serve

Directions

Place the shrimp in a bowl with 1 tablespoon of the oil, garlic, kosher salt and red chili flakes. Toss to coat and let marinade for 10 minutes.

Heat a 12-inch skillet over medium high heat with 1 tablespoon of oil. Add the onion and peppers and cook until softened, about 5 minutes. Transfer the peppers and onion to a bowl.

Add 1 tablespoon of oil and cook half of the shrimp for 2 minutes, then flip and cook for another 2 minutes or until opaque. Transfer the shrimp to a plate. Add the remaining tablespoon of oil and cook the remaining shrimp then add to the other shrimp.

In another bowl or 4 cup measuring cup mix the coconut milk, soy sauce, peanut butter, lime juice, brown sugar and ground ginger and stir well. Transfer the cooked onion and peppers to the skillet and pour the coconut milk mixture of the peppers. Bring to a boil then reduce to simmer and cook until reduced by half, about 5 minutes. Add the shrimp to the skillet with the basil and toss to coat.

Serve over rice.

Savory Roast Chicken with Sourkraut

Stuffing the chicken cavity with sourkraut leaves the chicken very moist.

Ingredients

  • 4-5 pound chicken
  • 2 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon pepper
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • 1 can sourkraut

Directions

Preheat oven to 350 degrees.

In a small bowl, combine all the spices.

Rub the outside of the chicken with olive oil. Take sourkraut and stuff the chicken cavity. Sprinkle the spice mixture over all parts of the chicken.

Place the chicken breast-side down in the pan, and cook for 20 minutes per pound, until interior temperature reaches 155 degrees.

Indian Cucumber Salad

This fresh tasting salad is excellent with curries.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 red onion, finely chopped
  • 2 pinches salt
  • 1 cucumber, finely chopped
  • juice from 1 lime
  • 2 tsp finely chopped cilantro

Directions

Place red onion in a non-reactive bowl and toss with the salt. Allow to sit 5 minutes. Rinse well and drain completely.

Add the cucumber, lime juice, cilantro. Stir to combine. Taste and adjust seasonings.

Asparagus with Lemon and Goat Cheese

The lemon compliments the ceamy goat cheese in this fresh spring salad.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound asparagus spears, trimmed
  • 1 1/2 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 2 tablespoons finely chopped chives
  • 2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice
  • 2 teaspoons olive oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 2 ounces creamy goat cheese

Directions

Preheat oven to 350 degrees. Line a cookie sheet with aluminum foil.

Drizzle olive oil over asparagus spears, and roast for 18 minutes.

Remove from heat, and cut into one and half inch pieces. Combine asparagus and tomatoes in a medium bowl.

Combine chives and next 5 ingredients (through salt), stirring with a whisk. Drizzle over the asparagus mixture, tossing gently to coat. Top with goat cheese.
